# Privacy Policy

**Effective Date:** June 30, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Verbly ("the App") collects, uses, and protects information when you use the application.

Verbly is developed and operated by Akash Shah ("Developer," "we," "our," or "us").

## Privacy First

Verbly is designed with privacy in mind.

Your vocabulary progress, saved words, preferences, onboarding status, and optional display name remain stored locally on your device. We do not require user accounts, and we do not synchronize your learning progress to our own servers.

## Information We Collect

### Information Stored Locally

The App may store the following information only on your device:

- Optional display name
- Saved vocabulary words
- Favorite words
- Learning progress
- Daily progress
- Word completion status
- Mastery levels
- User preferences
- Onboarding completion status
- Other settings necessary for normal operation of the App

This information is not uploaded to servers operated by the Developer.

### Analytics

To improve the App, Verbly uses PostHog to collect anonymous product analytics.

Examples of events may include:

- Session Started
- Free Word Completed
- Paywall Viewed
- Trial Started
- Subscription Started
- Card Answered
- Word Mastered

These events may include technical information such as device metadata and application version but are not intended to identify you personally.

### Subscriptions

Verbly offers optional premium subscriptions through Apple's App Store.

Subscription management is provided through RevenueCat.

RevenueCat and Apple may process information necessary to manage purchases, including:

- Subscription status
- Purchase receipts
- Product purchased
- Apple transaction identifiers
- Device identifiers required for subscription validation
- Billing-related metadata

The App does not intentionally send your email address, display name, phone number, or custom profile information to RevenueCat.

Payments are processed entirely by Apple. The Developer never receives your payment card information.

### Notification Permissions

Verbly may request permission to send notifications, such as reminders related to learning or practice.

You may decline or disable notifications at any time through your device settings.

## How Information Is Used

Information collected is used solely to:

- Operate the App
- Save your learning progress
- Unlock premium features
- Improve application performance
- Understand feature usage
- Fix issues and improve future versions

## Data Storage

Most user information remains stored locally on your device.

The Developer does not maintain cloud copies of your vocabulary progress, saved words, favorites, or preferences.

## Data Retention

Locally stored information remains on your device until:

- You delete it within the App, if applicable; or
- You uninstall the App.

Deleting the App generally removes locally stored learning data from your device.

Subscription records maintained by Apple and RevenueCat may continue to be retained according to their respective policies.

## Third-Party Services

Verbly uses third-party services including:

- Apple App Store
- Apple StoreKit
- RevenueCat
- PostHog

These providers maintain their own privacy policies and may process information according to their respective terms.

## Children's Privacy

Verbly has a 4+ age rating on the Apple App Store.

The App is intended for a general audience and is not designed to knowingly collect personal information from children.

If you believe information has been provided by a child in violation of applicable law, please contact us.

## Advertising

Verbly does not display third-party advertisements.

## Security

Reasonable measures are taken to protect the information processed by the App. However, no software or electronic storage system can be guaranteed to be completely secure.

## Your Choices

You may:

- Decline notification permissions
- Remove the App from your device
- Manage subscriptions through your Apple ID settings
- Contact us with privacy questions

Changes become effective upon publication of the updated version with a revised Effective Date.
